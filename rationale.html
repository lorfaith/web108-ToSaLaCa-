Rationale

Grocery stores face a significant challenge in managing food waste, with substantial quantities of edible products discarded due to near-expiry status, contributing to environmental degradation and resource depletion. This waste occurs despite the urgent need to address food insecurity in communities, highlighting a critical disconnect. The study aims to address these issues by developing a web-based platform that enables grocery stores to list near-expiry food items for discounted sale or donation, thereby reducing waste and promoting sustainability.

This platform streamlines the connection between grocery stores, consumers, and charities, ensuring safe and transparent food redistribution in compliance with regulatory standards like those of the FDA. By facilitating real-time notifications, location-based searches, and role-based access control, the system promotes efficient inventory management for stores, affordable access to food for consumers, and enhanced food availability for those in need, ultimately fostering more responsible and sustainable food practices.
